1. Reading

How to Network Effectively at a Professional Event

Networking is one of the most valuable career skills you can develop. At a job fair or professional event, the goal is not simply to collect business cards, but to build genuine connections with people who can help your career grow.

Preparation is essential. Before the event, research the companies attending and identify two or three you genuinely want to speak with. Prepare a brief, confident introduction and think of two follow-up questions that show curiosity and knowledge about the field.

During the event, approach each booth with confidence, maintain good eye contact, and listen actively. After the event, send a short, tailored follow-up message within 24 to 48 hours. The most memorable candidates are those who are prepared, engaged, and professional from the first handshake to the final email.
